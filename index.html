Why Gene Editing Should Be Used In The Medical Field

Remediation Project

By: Tyler McPhee

About The Author

In my senior year of high school, and I'm reading the book "Brave New World," which introduces me to a future where gene editing is commonplace. The idea of designing a fetus with "ideal" genes seems simultaneously fascinating and terrifying. Fast forward to today, and CRISPR is making headlines for its ability to tweak DNA sequences with incredible precision.

The Promise of Gene Editing in Medicine

CRISPR, a gene editing technology, has potential to revolutionize medicine by enabling precise genetic editing. In this vision, treatments could be customized to address genetic disorders at their root cause. This means diseases like cystic fibrosis and sickle cell anemia, which have been historically challenging to treat, could be effectively managed or even cured. The promise of CRISPR lies in its ability to bring hope to millions by offering the prospect of eradicating genetic diseases and significantly improving quality of life for those affected.

  • "CRISPR-Cas9 is a simple, inexpensive, and effective technique for gene editing with multiple applications from the development of functional genomics and epigenetics." - Choupina Ferreira

Realizing the Potential: Transformative Treatments and Hope

One of the most heartwarming stories I've come across involves Johnny (who is in the third picture), a teenager battling sickle cell disease. Despite frequent pain crises, his life took a dramatic turn when he became one of the youngest recipients of a CRISPR-based treatment. His remarkable transformation, freed from the constant fear of pain, symbolizes hope not only for him but for countless others facing similar battles. It illustrates the power of scientific advancements to bring relief and new possibilities to those grappling with genetic disorders.

  • "Before the treatment, Johnny's life was constrained by the constant fear of pain crises. Yet, with the CRISPR treatment, Johnny has regained a sense of normalcy." - CNN

Addressing Ethical Concerns

One pressing ethical dilemma is the potential for gene editing to exacerbate existing social inequalities. There's a legitimate fear that without careful regulation and oversight, genetic enhancements could be disproportionately available to the privileged few who can afford them, widening the gap between the haves and have-nots. This could lead to a future society where genetic advantages are concentrated among certain socio-economic classes, further entrenching disparities in health and opportunity.

  • "Without proactive measures to ensure equitable access to gene editing technologies, the promise of genetic advancements may remain out of reach for many." - Samuel Dickman

Proposing Solutions

Navigating the complex ethical waters of gene editing requires an approach that addresses both regulatory oversight and equitable access. Enhanced regulation and oversight are necessary to ensuring that gene editing technologies are deployed responsibly and ethically. This means establishing strong frameworks that govern the research, development, and clinical application of gene editing therapies. These frameworks should prioritize safety, efficacy, and adherence to ethical principles such as respect for autonomy, beneficence, and justice.

  • "Proposing measures to ensure equitable access to gene editing technologies, particularly for underprivileged communities, is crucial." - Sneha Gulati

Conclusion: Shaping the Future Responsibly

Embracing gene editing technology requires not only scientific prowess but also a deep sense of responsibility and compassion. We must proceed with caution, recognizing the weight of the decisions we make and the profound impact they can have on individuals, communities, and society as a whole. This means adhering to rigorous ethical standards, prioritizing safety and efficacy, and remaining vigilant against potential risks and unintended consequences. At the same time, we must approach gene editing with a sense of compassion, recognizing its potential to alleviate suffering and improve lives. By harnessing this technology responsibly, we can offer hope to millions of people affected by genetic diseases, disabilities, and other health challenges. Gene editing holds the promise of personalized medicine tailored to individual genetic profiles, offering targeted treatments with unprecedented precision and effectiveness.
